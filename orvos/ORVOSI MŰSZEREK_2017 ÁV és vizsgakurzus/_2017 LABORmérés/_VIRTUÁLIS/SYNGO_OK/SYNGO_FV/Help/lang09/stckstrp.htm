Displaying Images in Stack and Stripe Mode
Previous  Top  Next

Stack Mode:

 
 
In Stack mode, all of a series' images are stacked together. This is indicated by a dog-ear in the upper right corner of the image.  

Click on both halves of the dog-ear to browse through the stack (forward and backward). Alternatively, you can browse through the stack by turning the wheel on your wheel mouse.  
 
Some series may contain multiple frames, for example, images from a heart catheterization or ultrasound examination.  

In this case, each multiple frame image is assigned to the frames in the stack and the whole is displayed as a stack element. This means that a series with several multiple frame images is divided into several stacks.  
 
 
Stripe Mode:  
 
 
 
In Stripe mode, all of the images in a series are spread out flat and displayed next to each other in a similar way to the X-Rays you once received from your radiologist.  

 
You can switch between Stack mode and Stripe mode at any time.
